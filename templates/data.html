Looking at the data!

Data was taken from winemag.com, a wine enthusiast publication with descriptions, reviews and, most importantly, ratings for a wide variety of wines. Expert reviewers taste more than 24,000 wines from across the globe every year, covering everything from France and Italy to California, Australia, South Africa, Argentina and beyond and act as an extensive resource of information for all things wine. With just a quick look at the distribution of prices by points for around 130k bottles of wine, we can already see a couple of varieties standing out as being exceptional choices!

Taking a deeper dive and zooming in on specific regions, France for example, we see that bordeaux dominates the region of its namesake.

Surprisingly, however when we look at Champagne, it is in fact a Chardonnay that shows as the most expensive and highest rated wine from the data! This then begged the question: what is it about these particular wines that make them so special?

We can begin to extract the words that contribute to the overall quality of the wine. Flavors of buttered toast? Fruity? It appears these are distinguishing features of a Chardonnay!
